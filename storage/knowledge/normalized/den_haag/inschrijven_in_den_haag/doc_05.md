1st BRP registration when coming from abroad with residence permit (you do not have a BSN)
==========================================================================================

[Listen](https://app-eu.readspeaker.com/cgi-bin/rsent?voice=Alice&lang=en_uk&readclass=page-container__columns&customerid=13613&url=https://www.denhaag.nl/en/moving-and-immigration/1st-brp-registration-when-coming-from-abroad-with-residence-permit-you-do-not-have-a-bsn/ "Have the text read aloud with ReadSpeaker webReader")

Do you have the citizenship (nationality) of a country from outside the EU/EEA or Switzerland? Have you never previously lived in the Netherlands and will you be living in The Hague for 4 months or longer? Then you need to register with the municipality in the Personal Records Database (BRP). This is required by law.

Were you previously registered in the Netherlands? Or do you already have a citizen service number (BSN)? Or will you be staying for less than 4 months? Go to [Begin link: Register again in the BRP, end link.](https://www.denhaag.nl/en/moving-and-immigration/register-again-in-the-brp-you-already-have-a-bsn/)
 .

Conditions
----------

*   You have proof of lawful residence. This can be:
    *   a valid Dutch permanent residence document
    *   the residence endorsement sticker from the IND in your passport
    *   the temporary regular residence permit sticker (MVV)
    *   authorisation by the IND (Entrance and Residence procedure, TEV)
    *   the invitation by the IND to pick up the residence permit.
*   You will be living in The Hague for 4 months or longer.
*   You have not previously been registered in the Netherlands.
*   You do not yet have a [Begin link: citizen service number , end link.](https://www.denhaag.nl/en/passport-and-identity-card/burgerservicenummer-citizen-service-number/)
     (BSN).

Do you want to backdate your registration? This can be done on the date you made the appointment. Even if the appointment is later.

You then have:

*   made an appointment for a 1st registration or to register again (resettling)
*   made the appointment after you came to live in the Netherlands
*   a copy of the appointment confirmation with you (you will receive this by email. Print it or show it on your telephone, tablet or laptop. Please note! It must be clear to see when you made the appointment)

Needed at the appointment
-------------------------

*   A [Begin external link: valid form of identification(External link), end external link.](https://www.government.nl/topics/identification-documents/compulsory-identification)
     (this may not be a driving licence).
*   A proof of lawful residence (see [Begin link: Conditions, end link.](https://www.denhaag.nl/en/moving-and-immigration/moving-to-the-hague-from-abroad/brp-registration-when-coming-from-abroad-you-do-not-have-a-bsn/1st-brp-registration-when-coming-from-abroad-with-residence-permit-you-do-not-have-a-bsn/#conditions)
     ).
*   A [Begin link: proof of occupancy, end link.](https://www.denhaag.nl/en/moving-and-immigration/proof-of-occupancy/)
     .
*   All family members who would like to register need to attend the appointment. This includes babies, young children and older family members.

If you were born, married or divorced abroad, also take the following documents to the appointment:

*   Your birth certificate
*   If applicable: the birth certificates of your partner and/or your children
*   If applicable: your marriage certificate or certificate of civil partnership
*   If applicable: divorce papers
*   If applicable: adoption papers and/or a death certificate for your partner or child(ren)

These documents are not required for registration. Nevertheless take them with you to your appointment. The municipality will review these documents. If they are in order, the information will be registered in the BRP.

You need to have certain foreign certificates legalised (made official with a signature and stamp/’apostille’). More information can be found on the [Begin external link: website of the Government of the Netherlands(External link), end external link.](https://www.netherlandsworldwide.nl/legalisation)
. Choose the country which issued your document.

Make an appointment
-------------------

### Online

[Make an appointment(External link)](https://denhaag.mijnafspraakmaken.nl/?product=30)

### By telephone

Would you like an appointment for you and your family? Call the municipality on tel. 14070 to make an appointment. From abroad you can call tel. +31 70 353 30 00 to make an appointment.

Bring your documents to the Public Service counter at the [Begin link: Segbroek district office, end link.](https://www.denhaag.nl/en/contact-with-the-municipality/city-district-offices-opening-hours-and-locations/#addresses-and-opening-hours)
 .

Make sure that you are on time for your appointment. If you arrive too late, there may be no time to help you.

You need to make an appointment at [Begin external link: The Hague International Centre(External link), end external link.](https://www.thehagueinternationalcentre.nl/make-an-appointment-for-registration)
 if:

*   you are a recognised scientific researcher
*   you fall under the [Begin link: highly-skilled migrant scheme, end link.](https://www.denhaag.nl/en/moving-and-immigration/brp-registration-for-highly-skilled-migrants-and-scientific-researchers-coming-from-abroad/)
     of the Immigration and Naturalisation Service (IND)
*   you have a [Begin link: ‘privileged’ status, end link.](https://www.denhaag.nl/en/moving-and-immigration/brp-registration-for-privileged-persons/)
     in the Netherlands

Fees
----

Free.

How long it takes
-----------------

Your registration will usually be processed in the BRP within 4 weeks. You will receive a confirmation of your registration with an overview of your registered information and your citizen service number (BSN) by post.

Good to know
------------

*   Have your birth certificate, marriage certificate, divorce papers, adoption certificate(s) or other important documents not (yet) been registered in the BRP? Bring the documents to your appointment. If you do not yet have the documents, you can submit them at a later moment at an appointment. Read more on the page [Begin link: Register foreign documents in the BRP., end link.](https://www.denhaag.nl/en/moving-and-immigration/register-foreign-documents-in-the-brp/)

*   Would you like to know which personal data can be found in the Dutch population register (BRP) and what they are used for? Read about this on the [Begin external link: website of the Government of the Netherlands(External link), end external link.](https://www.government.nl/topics/personal-data/personal-records-database-brp)
    .
*   Will you be moving to another address in The Hague after you have registered? [Begin link: Report your move, end link.](https://www.denhaag.nl/en/moving-and-immigration/report-your-move/)
     .
*   Will you be moving to another municipality in the Netherlands or abroad after you have registered? [Begin link: Report your move to the new municipality or deregister, end link.](https://www.denhaag.nl/en/moving-and-immigration/deregister-from-the-municipality/)
     .
